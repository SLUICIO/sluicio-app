# Build-in-public — 15-minute time-to-value with a customer

**Pillar:** Build-in-Public (proof / time-to-value) · **Channel:** LinkedIn (personal profile)
**Goal:** concrete proof-of-value; strongest design-partner hook yet

## LinkedIn version

Feature work is up and down — build, test, verify, repeat. Some weeks you wonder if it's landing. Then you get a moment that reminds you why.

This week I ran a live test instance of Sluicio with a customer. I've never seen onboarding move this fast:

→ 5 minutes in — we could see every integration actually running.
→ 10 minutes in — we understood how some of them were behaving.
→ 15 minutes in — we'd found a real issue in one of them.

Fifteen minutes. From "let's plug it in" to "…wait, look at that."

That's the whole reason to build on OpenTelemetry and obsess over onboarding: time-to-value measured in minutes, not nights.

Still beta. Still plenty to optimize — but now we can see exactly where. This was the week the idea felt real.
